Megan Bates is a computational artist who works largely with the mediums of code, 3d modelling and paint to produce works about our increasingly complex relationship with machines. Her physics background informs her practice, and she is particularly interested in entanglement/interconnectedness in its various forms, both spiritual and measurable.

Currently, she works largely with imagined spaces, collaborated between herself and her PC. Renders allow Megan to create photorealistic lighting within impossible environments, and enable her to construct dreamlike worlds intertwining unusual materials to paint liquid pools, misty fogs, and fleshy appendages.

She takes inspiration from dutch flower paintings- sensuous from a distance, but challenging and disorienting on closer inspection. Her queer experience informs the politics of depicting scenes of ugly but loveable androgynous critters.
